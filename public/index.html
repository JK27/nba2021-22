PLAYERS BY POSITION

This chart distributes the players according to their primary position on the court, the position they normally play as.

Shooting Guard (SG) is the most populated one as it is also the most versatile, these players can usually cover the postions of Point Guard (PG) and Small Forward (SF).

Center (C) is the position that has the less number of players as these are the tallest players and this position sometimes is occupied by Power Forward players (PF).

PLAYERS BY COUNTRY

About 75% of players are American. Despite the number of players from other countries increasing year by year, especially in the last two decades, these only reach just about a quarter of the total.

Los Angeles Lakers is the only team of the 2018/19 season with only American born players in its team roster.

Dallas Mavericks is the team with the most variety of nationalities, with players coming from nine different countries and four continents: USA, Tunisia, Latvia, Germany, Canada, Puerto Rico, Australia, Greece and Slovenia.

PLAYERS FROM THE REST OF THE WORLD

Despite still being a clear minority when compared to players form the USA, there are players from all continents (except Antarctica), and their number have been increasing with the years with more and more being traded in or drafted every year.

Europe is still the dominant market for non-American players, but Canada and Australia are the individual countries with more players in the 2018/19 season.

PLAYERS BY STATE (USA and CANADA only)

There are players from the majority of the US States. The only states with no players representing them on the NBA during season 2018/19 are: Alaska, Hawaii, Idaho, Maine, Montana, New Mexico, Rhode Island, South Dakota and Vermont.

From the 16 Canadian players currently playing in the league, 13 of them come from the province of Ontario, where the only Canadian team in the League, the Toronto Raptors, are based in.

PLAYERS BY AGE

The ample majority of players are aged between 20 and 26 years old, being 25 the average age from all players in the league. Due to the high level of physicality of the game, the number of players in their thirties is clearly low when compared to those in their twenties, number that reduces dramatically for over 35 years old, with only eleven players above this age.

Fun fact: there are 39 current players that were not born yet when Vince Carter and Dirk Nowitzki, the two most experienced players in the league, started their careers on the NBA back in 1998.

Youngest player: Talen Horton-Tucker

Date of birth: 25 - November - 2000

Oldest player: Vince Carter

Date of birth: 26 - January - 1977

PLAYERS BY DRAFT YEAR (AGE v EXPERIENCE)

When talking about basketball, and sports in general, experience is not everything that matters. The most experienced team in the league, the Los Angeles Lakers, did not even got through to the play-offs, finishing the regular season on the 20th postition out of 30 teams.

There are only 13 players on the 2018/19 season with 15 years or more of experience since they were rookies in the league. Curiously, there is no players that were drafted in 1999.

Most experienced team: Los Angeles Lakers

Average player experience: 7 seasons

Least experienced team: Chicago Bulls

Average player experience: 3 seasons
